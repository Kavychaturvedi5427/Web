What are HTML Entities?

HTML Entities are used to represent special characters in a way that the browser can understand . They begins with ampersand (&) and ends with a semicolon (;).

Why use HTML Entities?

They are generally used to write those thing which might collide with HTML syntax. Here are some reasons to use HTML Entities :-

  • Reserved Characters: Characters like <,>, and & are reserved in HTML
  • Special Symbols: For symbols like ©, ®, or mathematical symbols.
  • Non-Breaking Spaces: To create white spaces that won't break into a new line.

If we want to tell someone in webpage that how paragraph tag looks like then we can't just simply write p with less than and greater than symbol . In such cases we use HTML Entities.

This is a paragraph opening tag <p> and this is a paragraph closing tag </p>.

Few other examples of HTML Entities :-
  • ampersand(lt); for <
  • ampersand(gt); for >
  • ampersand(amp); for &
  • ampersand(nbsp); for a non-breaking space
  • ampersand(copy); for ©
